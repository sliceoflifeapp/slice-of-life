GETTING STARTED
What kind of footage does this work with?
Any video you shoot on your iPhone, Android, DSLR, or drone. The more variety the better — a mix of you talking to camera and b-roll of what's happening around you gives the best results.
Do I need to talk to camera?
No — turn on Highlight Reel mode and the app will cut together your best b-roll shots automatically. But if you do talk to camera, even just for 30–60 seconds, your vlog will have a narrative backbone that makes it feel like a real story.
How long should my clips be?
Anything works. Clips under 2 seconds are skipped automatically. For narration, 30 seconds to a few minutes is ideal. For b-roll, 5–15 seconds per shot gives the best material to work with.
What file formats are supported?
MP4, MOV, M4V, AVI, MKV, MTS, M2TS, and WEBM. If your iPhone, DSLR, or camera shot it, it almost certainly works.
THE OUTPUT
Why did some of my clips get cut out?
The app scores every clip for quality — brightness, sharpness, and visual interest. Clips that are too dark, blurry, or very short get filtered out automatically. Use Director's Notes on the configure screen to tell it what to include or skip.
Does it work for multi-day footage?
Yes — just drop in the folder. Slice handles it automatically. But the app is designed around daily vlogging — one shoot, one Slice, every day.
TECHNICAL
Does my footage leave my computer?
Your original video files never leave your machine. To analyze your clips, small frame samples are sent to the cloud for AI processing. For narration, short transcript excerpts are also processed. Your raw footage stays local at all times.
Why is it taking so long to render?
Render time depends on how much footage you have and its resolution. 4K footage is significantly slower than 1080p. A typical render takes 3–10 minutes. Multi-day folders take longer because each day is processed individually.
EDITING
Can I edit the result in Premiere or Final Cut?
Yes — every render automatically exports an XML file to your Downloads folder. Open it in Premiere Pro or Final Cut Pro to get a fully laid-out vlog timeline with your original high-quality source clips, ready to refine.
What is the XML export for?
It gives editors a head start. Instead of sorting through raw footage yourself, you get a rough cut of your vlog in your editing software with all clips placed and synced. Tweak from there.
Can I re-render with different settings?
Yes. On the results screen after any render, there's a Re-edit button. Change the orientation, caption style, or other settings and re-render in seconds — without waiting for the AI to re-analyze your footage.
ACCOUNT
Can I use my own Anthropic API key?
Yes. Go to Settings and paste your key in the API Key field. The app will use it for all AI processing.

How to vlog for best results
Act 1
Open to camera
Film a 30–60 second talking head to open. Where are you? What's the plan for today? This becomes your hook.
Act 2
Shoot your b-roll
Film the places, food, people, and action around you. The more variety the better — these become your cutaways.
Act 3
Close to camera
Talk to camera at the end. What happened? What's the takeaway? A strong close gives your vlog a real ending instead of just a fade out.
Tip: Slice of Life detects when you're talking to camera and treats those clips as the backbone of your vlog. B-roll fills in around them automatically.
Before you start: Copy your footage to your Mac first. Don't run Slice of Life directly off an SD card or camera — if the connection drops mid-render, your export will fail.
