FAQ
GETTING STARTED
What kind of footage does this work with?
Any video you shoot on your iPhone, Android, DSLR, or drone. The more variety the better — a mix of you talking to camera and b-roll of what's happening around you gives the best results.
Do I need to talk to camera?
No — turn on Highlight Reel mode and the app will cut together your best b-roll shots automatically. But if you do talk to camera, even just for 30–60 seconds, your Slice will have a narrative backbone that makes it feel like a real story.
How long should my clips be?
Anything works. Clips under 2 seconds are skipped automatically. For narration, 30 seconds to a few minutes is ideal. For b-roll, 5–15 seconds per shot gives the best material to work with.
What file formats are supported?
MP4, MOV, M4V, AVI, MKV, MTS, M2TS, and WEBM. If your iPhone, DSLR, or camera shot it, it almost certainly works.
THE OUTPUT
Why did some of my clips get cut out?
The app scores every clip for quality — brightness, sharpness, and visual interest. Clips that are too dark, blurry, or very short get filtered out automatically. Use Director's Notes on the configure screen to tell it what to include or skip.
What's the difference between Single Day and Trip?
Single Day is for one day of footage — it builds a single narrative arc. Trip is for multi-day adventures — it groups footage by calendar day and builds a story arc across all days.
TECHNICAL
Does my footage leave my computer?
No. Your video files never leave your machine. The only thing sent to the cloud is a small thumbnail frame from each clip — used to analyse what's in the shot. Your originals stay local at all times.
Why is it taking so long to render?
Render time depends on how much footage you have and its resolution. 4K footage is significantly slower than 1080p. A typical single-day render takes 3–10 minutes. Trip mode takes longer because it processes multiple days.
EDITING
Can I edit the result in Premiere or Final Cut?
Yes — every render automatically exports an XML file to your Downloads folder. Open it in Premiere Pro or Final Cut Pro to get a fully laid-out timeline with your original high-quality source clips, ready to refine.
What is the XML export for?
It gives editors a head start. Instead of sorting through raw footage yourself, you get a rough cut in your editing software with all clips placed and synced. Tweak from there.
Can I re-render with different settings?
Yes. On the results screen after any render, there's a Re-edit button. Change the orientation, caption style, or other settings and re-render in seconds — without waiting for the AI to re-analyse your footage.
ACCOUNT
Can I use my own Anthropic API key?
Yes. Go to Settings and paste your key in the API Key field. The app will use it for all AI processing.

How to film your day
Act 1
Set the scene
Start with a 30–60 second clip talking to camera. Who are you with? Where are you? What's happening today? This becomes your opening narration.
Act 2
Capture the moments
Let the camera roll on what's actually happening — food, places, people, action. Narrate in the moment as things unfold. Don't overthink it.
Act 3
Reflect
Before you wrap, talk to camera for 1–2 minutes. What happened? What surprised you? What will you remember? This is the emotional anchor of your Slice.
Tip: Slice of Life detects when you're talking to camera and treats those clips as the backbone of your video. B-roll fills in around them automatically.
Before you start: Copy your footage to your Mac first. Don't run Slice of Life directly off an SD card or camera — if the connection drops mid-render, your export will fail.
